Gluten Free Lasagna

Ingredients

  • 5oz gf Noodles
  • 1 lb turkey
  • 1 cup chopped onions
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 8oz tomato sauce
  • 8 oz shredded mozzarella
  • 6 oz tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon dried basil
  • 1 tablespoon dried parsley
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 egg whisked
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • ½ cup grated parmesan

Prep

  1. Boil water
    1. Add pasta when ready
  2. Chop the onions
  3. Mince the garlic
  4. Preheat oven to 375f
  5. Oil baking pan
  6. Combine onions and garlic along with spices
  7. Mix ricotta with egg and parmesan cheese and parsley

Directions

  1. Heat saucepan
  2. Add meat, onions, garlic and spices
  3. Break meat apart as the dish cooks
  4. Add tomatoes once meat is cooked
    1. Tomato sauce, paste, and crushed
  5. Bring sauce to a boil
  6. Simmer for 5 minutes
    1. Stir!
  7. Layer the pan
    1. Meat sauce
    2. Pasta
    3. Cheese
    4. Meat sauce
    5. Pasta cheese
    6. Top with mozzarella
  8. Bake for 30-35 minutes
  9. Rest for ~10 minutes

Tom Kha Soup

Notes

Time 

Servings

 Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • ½ of one onion sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic chopped
  • ½ of one red jalapeno pepper sliced, or a couple Thai chiles, halved
  • 3 ¼-inch slices galangal or ginger
  • 1 lemongrass stalk pounded with the side of a knife and cut into 2-inch long pieces
  • 2 teaspoons red Thai curry paste
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 4 cups canned coconut cream or coconut milk
  • 2 medium chicken breasts cut into bite-sized pieces (or shrimp)
  • 8 ounces white mushroom caps sliced
  • 1 ½ – 2 tablespoons fish sauce plus more to taste
  • 2-3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 2-3 green onions sliced thin
  • fresh cilantro chopped, for garnish

Instructions 

  1. Medium pot heat Coconut oil
  2. Add 
    • Onion
    • Garlic
    • Jalapeno
    • Ginger
    • Lemongrass
    • Red curry paste
  3. Heat for 5 minutes stirring frequently
  4. Add chicken broth
  5. Bring to boil
  6. Reduce heat
  7. Simmer (uncovered) for 30 minutes
  8. Strain out the aromatics (the garlic, onions, lemongrass, and ginger) and discard. 
  9. Add in 
    • coconut milk
    • chicken breast (or tofu or shrimp)
    • mushrooms. 
  10. Simmer until chicken breast pieces are just cooked through
  11. add 
    • fish sauce or ½ tbsp of salt
    • coconut aminos (or coconut sugar)
    • lime juice, plus more of each to taste. 
  12. Cook 2 minutes
  13. ladle into serving bowls 
  14. top with sliced green onions and fresh cilantro.

To use shrimp, simply stir in 1 pound of raw shrimp instead of chicken. Simmer until just cooked through, pink, and no longer translucent.

Lettuce Cups

Ingredients

  • Olive oil 1 tablespoon 
  • Ground chicken 1 pound
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 onion diced
  • ¼ cup hoisin sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine vinegar 
  • 1 tablespoon fresh grated ginger 
  • 1 tablespoon sriracha 
  • 1 8 oz can whole water chestnuts 
  • 2 green onions 
  • 1 head butter lettuce 

Directions

  1. Heat olive oil over medium heat 
  2. Add ground chicken 
  3. cook until browned, 3-5 minutes, crumbling meat 
  4. Drain the fat
  5. Stir in garlic, onion, hoisin, soy sauce, rice wine vinegar, ginger, and sriracha.
  6. Stir fry for 2 minutes.
  7. Stir in chestnuts, green onions 
  8. Cook until tender, 1-2 minutes or more
  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste
  10. Serve taco style with lettuce leafs.

Mashed Potatoes

Ingredients

  • 5lbs potatoes
  • 2 large cloves garlic
  • Fine sea salt
  • 6 tbsp butter
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 4 ounces cream cheese
  • Toppings
    • Chives
    • Green onions
    • Black pepper

Prep

  1. Cut potatoes into inch thick chunks
  2. Fill pot with cold water
  3. Put potatoes in pot
  4. Water should be 1” above the potatoes

Directions

  1. Stir in garlic and 1 tbsp of salt
  2. Turn heat to high until boiling
  3. Reduce heat but still boil for 10-12 minutes
    1. Knife should slide into potatoes easily
  4. Separate pot
    1. Add butter milk and 2 tbsp salt
  5. Heat until butter is just melted
  6. Set aside
    1. Wait for potatoes
  7. Drain out water from potatoes
  8. Return potatoes to pot w/o water and heat
  9. Shake pot gently for 1 min
  10.  Turn off heat
  11. Mash the potatoes
  12. Stir in butter mixture
  13. Serve

Cookies!

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups (~282 grams) good all-purpose gluten-free flour
  • 1/2 tsp. xanthan gum* (see note)
  • 1 tsp. baking soda
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 2 ounces cream cheese, room temp
  • 3/4 cups (12 Tbsp.) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 1/2 tsp. pure vanilla extract
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 2 cups (12 oz.) semi-sweet chocolate chips

Directions

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together gluten-free flour, xanthan (unless the flour blend already has xanthan or guar gum), baking soda and salt. Set aside.
  2. In the bowl of your stand mixer, place the cream cheese, then pour melted butter over it. Add brown sugar and sugar and mix on medium speed for 2 minutes. (I use the paddle attachment on my mixer.)
  3. Add vanilla extract and egg yolks (one at a time) mixing on low-medium speed until well mixed.
  4. Add the flour mixture that you set aside earlier, beating on low until just combined.
  5. Add the chocolate chips and mix on low or by hand, just until mixed thoroughly.
  6. Cover the mixing bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate a minimum of 4 hours and up to 4 days.
  7. When you are ready to bake, remove from refrigerator and allow it to come closer to room temperature so you can scoop the cookies easily before baking.
  8. Preheat the oven to 375°.
  9. Line cookie sheets with sheets of parchment paper or silicone liners. Do not spray!
  10. Use a #20 cookie scoop to scoop even mounds of cookie dough spaced several inches apart. I can get 12 per cookie sheet.
  11. Bake the cookies for 11-12 minutes at 375°. Remove when edges are set and just brown. The centers will look undercooked, but will continue cooking as they cool. To ensure you don't over bake, I suggest you bake a few test cookies so you can determine the right baking time for your oven. If you like gooey centers, cook less, if you like crunchier cookies, cook longer.
  12. If you'd like, you can sprinkle more chocolate chips on them once you remove them from the oven.
  13. Let the cookies sit on the cookie sheet for just 2-3 minutes before removing to a cooling rack to finish cooling.

Buddha Bowl

Ingredients 

Bowl

  • ¾ cup uncooked quinoa
  • 2 large carrots (peeled & chopped)
  • 1 red onion (chopped into 1 inch pieces)
  • 2 cups brussels sprouts ( outer leaves removed and cut in half)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • salt & pepper
  • 19 oz can of chickpeas (drained; 15 or 19 oz can)

Dresing

  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 2 teaspoons maple syrup
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice
  • salt

Prep

Cook quinoa according to package directions. Portion out into four 2-cup capacity storage containers and allow to cool.

Heat oven to 425°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment and set aside.

Toss carrots, onion, brussels sprouts in olive oil and season with salt & pepper. 

Spread out on the baking sheet and bake in the oven for 15-20 minutes, stirring halfway through, or until veggies are soft.

While veggies are baking, shake together all tahini dressing ingredients. 

Between the four meal prep containers, portion out the chickpeas, veggies, and tahini sauce (you can drizzle right away or add it to a condiment container to add fresh).

Storage

Store in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat until steaming hot, or enjoy cold.
